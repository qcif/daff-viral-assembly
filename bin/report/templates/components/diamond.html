Homology search with DIAMOND Blastx
  1. Assembled contigs are queried with DIAMOND blastx against the RVDB protein database to identify viral protein-level homology.
  2. Sort the table by alphabetical or numerical order by clicking on column headers, and adjust the number of displayed entries using the dropdown menu above the table.
  3. The results can be limited to a specific virus genus or species by typing its name in the search box.
  4. Results are initially sorted by bitscore in descending order to prioritize the strongest matches.
  5. Because DIAMOND blastx aligns translated protein sequences against nucleotide contigs, query coverage percentage is calculated by multiplying the alignment length by three to approximate nucleotide coverage across the assembled contig.
  6. Only blastx hits with query coverage and percent identity of 50% or higher are displayed in the table.
  7. Please note that while blastx can identify more distant homology at the protein level, it may also yield more false positives as these sequences are only compared to the RVDB database. Always consider the full context of the results when interpreting blastx hits.
