Contig-based and homology-supported viral evidence summary

The table below summarises candidate viruses and viroids detected by the pipeline for which at least one de novo assembled contig showed homology and passed both the coverage- and term-based filters. Where multiple contigs were assigned to the same virus or viroid species, a single representative contig is displayed (see Section 5 for details). The table also reports the total number of conserved viral domains identified within each contig using HMMER (hmmscan), including whether an RNA-dependent RNA polymerase (RdRP) domain was detected. In addition, supporting evidence from direct taxonomic read classification (Kraken and Kaiju) is provided for the corresponding taxa. More detailed results, sequence alignments and visualisations are presented in the following sections of the report.

Novel contig evidence summary

This table is intended to highlight the most promising novel viral candidates, supported by at least two lines of evidence from Megablast, Diamond and geNomad (please refer to the Novel virus detection tab), for further investigation and validation. The table reports the viral taxonomic assignments recovered from nucleotide- and protein-based homology searches, associated sequence identity values, GeNomad viral prediction scores, predicted RNA-dependent RNA polymerase (RdRp) status, number of predicted open reading frames (ORFs), number of detected viral PFAM domains, and the number of predicted viral hallmark genes (derived from hmmscan). In addition, the table reports whether related unresolved viral taxa were independently detected by Kraken2 or Kaiju, providing further evidence supporting the presence of potentially novel or highly divergent viruses within the sample.
