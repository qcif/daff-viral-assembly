Novel virus detection
  1. De novo assembled contigs that are at least 500 bp in length and did not show significant similarity to any sequences in the NCBI core_nt database were further analysed to identify potential novel viruses.
  2. Contigs were classified usinggeNomad, a machine learning–based tool that evaluates gene content and sequence composition to determine viral origin. The table displays contigs retained as high-confidence candidate viral sequences, defined by a virus score (posterior probability of being viral) of at least 0.999.
  3. The number of viral hallmark genes detected by geNomad within a contig is also reported.
  4. Open reading frames (ORFs) encoding protein sequences of at least 100 amino acids were then predicted from these contigs using orfipy.
  5. The predicted protein sequences were subsequently searched against the curated Pfam-A profile Hidden Markov Model database using hmmscan.
  6. The table reports the number of ORFs with a significant match to a viral protein family domain (defined as a full sequence score of at least 50), the total number of viral domains detected, and whether an RNA-dependent RNA polymerase (RdRP) domain was identified.
  7. Rows are sorted in descending order based on the contigs virus score, with higher-scoring contigs representing higher-confidence viral candidates.
